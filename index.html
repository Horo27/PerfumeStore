FAQs

What is the difference between Eau de Parfum, Eau de Toilette, and Eau de Cologne?

The difference lies in the concentration of fragrance oils:

  • Eau de Parfum (EDP): Contains 15-20% fragrance oil and lasts around 6-8 hours.
  • Eau de Toilette (EDT): Contains 5-15% fragrance oil and lasts about 4-6 hours.
  • Eau de Cologne (EDC): Has a lower concentration (2-5%) and lasts about 2-3 hours.
How should I store my perfumes to make them last longer?

To preserve your perfume's longevity, store it in a cool, dark place away from direct sunlight and heat sources. Avoid keeping it in the bathroom, as humidity can alter the fragrance composition.

Do perfumes expire?

Yes, perfumes have a shelf life. Most last between 3 to 5 years if stored properly. Signs of expiration include a change in color, a weaker scent, or a sour smell.

How can I choose the right perfume for my personality and style?

Perfume selection depends on personal preference and occasions:

  • Fresh & Citrusy: Ideal for energetic and outdoorsy individuals.
  • Floral & Romantic: Great for feminine, elegant personalities.
  • Woody & Spicy: Suits sophisticated and bold individuals.
  • Oriental & Exotic: Perfect for mysterious and sensual vibes.

Test perfumes on your skin before purchasing, as they can smell different depending on body chemistry.

Can I layer different perfumes?

Yes! Layering allows you to create a unique scent. Start with a base fragrance (like a musk or vanilla), then add a complementary top note (such as citrus or floral). Avoid mixing too many strong scents to prevent clashing.
